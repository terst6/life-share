<h2>Plastic particle risk management</h2>
  <p>Latest research has found that significant amounts of microplastic particles have accumulated in the human brain. In brain samples from some Parkinson’s patients, alarming amounts of microplastics accumulated. Unlike plastic particles that are ingested by mouth and can be metabolized in the digestive tract, particles inhaled into the nasal cavity through the air can bypass the blood-brain barrier and directly enter the brain and form permanent accumulation.
  </p><p>The most serious source at present may be daily bedding: polyester fiber pillows (such as fiber cotton, thermal cotton), memory foam pillows and mattresses. A few years ago, the public lacked awareness of microplastic pollution, resulting in maternal and infant products relying heavily on plastic materials. Except for food-grade silicone and a few materials with stable properties, most plastic products will release particles during the aging process.
  </p><p>Lifestyle needs to be reshaped. For example, when sleeping on a foam mattress and using a fiber pillow, the tiny vibrations from each breath can cause the fibers to break and flake off and be inhaled at close range for up to 8 hours of sleep.
  </p><p>Suggested adjustments:
  <br>• Change bedding: switch to pure cotton or silk bedding. For pillows, buckwheat husk and other natural fillings are recommended.
  <br>• Go plastic: Replace plastic crispers in refrigerators and kitchens with borosilicate glass or 316L stainless steel.
  <bt>• Be wary of the air ducts of home appliances: behind the high-speed fans of air purifiers and air conditioners are usually plastic fiber filters. Under long-term high-speed airflow erosion, these fibers will be polished into fine particles and ejected.
  <br>• Eating habits: Reduce high-temperature stir-frying and grilling. The high incidence of lung cancer among women who do not smoke is largely related to the logical closed loop formed by kitchen fumes and indoor air particles.
  <br>• Use cotton masks for masks and avoid plastic products in the nose
  </p>

 <h2>塑料颗粒风险管控</h2>
  <p>最新研究发现，人类大脑中已积累了显著含量的微塑料颗粒。在部分帕金森患者的脑部样本中，微塑料的累积量惊人。与经口摄入、可随消化道代谢的塑料微粒不同，通过空气吸入鼻腔的微粒可绕过血脑屏障，直接进入大脑并形成永久性累积。
  </p><p>目前最严峻的来源可能是日常寝具：聚酯纤维枕（如纤维棉、保暖棉）、记忆棉枕头及床垫。几年前，公众对微塑料污染尚缺乏认知，导致母婴产品大量依赖塑料材质。除食品级硅胶及少数几种性质稳定的材质外，大多数塑料制品在老化过程中都会释放微粒。
  </p><p>生活方式需重塑。例如，睡在泡沫床垫上并使用纤维枕时，每一次呼吸产生的微小震动都会导致纤维断裂剥落，并在长达 8 小时的睡眠中被近距离吸入。
  </p><p>建议调整：
  <br>• 更换寝具：改用纯棉、真丝床上用品，枕头推荐荞麦壳等天然填充物。
  <br>• 去塑料化：将冰箱及厨房中的塑料保鲜盒更换为高硼硅玻璃或 316L 不锈钢。
  <bt>• 警惕家电风道：空气净化器与空调的高速风扇背后通常贴有塑料纤维过滤棉，在长期高速气流冲刷下，这些纤维会被打磨成细微颗粒喷出。
  <br>• 饮食习惯：减少高温爆炒及烧烤。女性不吸烟却高发肺癌，很大程度上与厨房油烟及室内空气微粒形成的逻辑闭环有关。
  <br>• 口罩使用棉布口罩，避免鼻内塑料用品
  </p>
